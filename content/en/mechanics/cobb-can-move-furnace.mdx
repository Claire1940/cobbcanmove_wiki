<Section icon={Target} title="Furnace Mechanics and Core Objectives">
The furnace in *Cobb Can Move* serves as the core objective, driving the player's survival loop. Your primary goal in many levels is to locate and fuel this furnace with coal to maintain light and progress. Without a functioning furnace, the darkness sets in, increasing Cobb's threat and making survival significantly harder.

Recent updates have refined how the furnace interacts with other game elements, particularly coal placement and the ending sequence. These changes aim to enhance the challenge and strategic depth of the game.

<Section icon={Map} title="Coal Collection and Routing Strategies">
Coal is the essential fuel for the furnace. Its collection and efficient delivery are paramount to survival. The game's procedural generation means coal spawns vary, requiring players to adapt their routing strategies constantly.

Historically, coal might spawn conveniently close to the furnace. However, the v1.6 update specifically made coal less likely to appear directly adjacent to the furnace, demanding more movement and strategic planning from players. This change significantly impacts early-game pressure and routing decisions.

<Callout type="warning">
Be aware of Cobb's sensory rules (e.g., "Cobb can smell") when planning your coal routes. A direct path might expose you to Cobb's detection.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Blast Furnace in the Ending Sequence">
The furnace takes on a critical, albeit slightly different, role in the ending sequence of *Cobb Can Move*. Here, it transforms into a "blast furnace," serving as the final interaction point after completing other objectives, such as placing all four batteries.

The developers recognized that player interaction with this crucial end-game object needed to be smoother. The v1.6 update addressed this by enlarging the blast furnace's hitbox. This change ensures that players are less likely to struggle with interacting with it, especially during the intense final moments of a run.

<Callout type="info">
The enlarged hitbox for the blast furnace in the ending sequence (v1.6) is a quality-of-life improvement designed to prevent frustrating misses during high-pressure scenarios.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Optimizing Furnace Operations for Survival">
Mastering the furnace in *Cobb Can Move* goes beyond simply feeding it coal. It involves a strategic understanding of resource management, enemy behavior, and environmental awareness. Here's a step-by-step guide to optimizing your furnace operations:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Coal Early">
    In the initial stages of any run, your main goal should be to secure a steady supply of coal for the furnace. Scout nearby areas carefully, always keeping an eye on Cobb's movements.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Establish Efficient Coal Routes">
    Once you've identified coal spawns, plan the safest and quickest routes to transport it back to the furnace. Avoid areas with high Cobb activity if possible. Remember, coal spawns are less predictable now.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Distractions">
    If Cobb is too close to your coal supply or the furnace, use throwable items like rocks to create a distraction. A direct hit can briefly stun Cobb, giving you a window to act.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Light Levels">
    The furnace provides light. Keep it fueled to maintain visibility, which is crucial for avoiding Cobb and navigating the procedurally generated maps. Low light significantly increases the danger.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Adapt to Rule Changes">
    Each run introduces changing rules. Some rules might affect Cobb's senses, making your trips to and from the furnace more perilous. Always read the active rule text and adjust your strategy accordingly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
Consider creating a small "safe zone" or temporary cache for coal near the furnace if the map layout permits. This minimizes exposure during crucial fueling moments.
