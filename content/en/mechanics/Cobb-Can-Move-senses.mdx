The "Cobb Can Smell" rule introduces a different layer of threat, as Cobb can track players by scent. This ability bypasses visual and auditory hiding spots, forcing players to manage their presence in a new way.

| Scent Detection Aspect | Description                                                                        | Official Update Note (v1.7)                                     | Player Takeaway                                               |
| :--------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Detection Mechanism**  | Cobb tracks player scent, independent of line of sight or sound.                   | Nerfed with less frequent sniffs and less persistence at close range. | Less relentless than before, but still a significant threat.  |
| **Persistence**        | Scent trails can linger, allowing Cobb to follow your past path.                   | Reduced persistence at close range.                              | Moving quickly out of an area can reduce tracking effectiveness. |

| **Counterplay**        | No direct counter besides continuous movement and avoiding prolonged presence.      | Focus on rapid movement and environmental changes.              | Constant repositioning is key; don't rely on static hiding.   |

The v1.7 update specifically targeted "Cobb Can Smell," making it less oppressive. This means players have a slightly larger window to escape detection once scented, but it remains a potent threat.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Cobb Can Hear: Responding to Sound Cues">
The "Cobb Can Hear" rule makes noise a critical factor in your survival. Every step, item interaction, or dropped object can alert Cobb to your position, demanding extreme caution and silent movement.

| Sound Source      | Effect on Cobb's Hearing | Player Strategy                                          | Update Significance (v1.6)                                 |
| :---------------- | :----------------------- | :------------------------------------------------------- | :--------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Player Footsteps** | Louder movement increases detection range. | Move slowly; avoid running unless absolutely necessary.  | No specific updates, but base sound mechanics apply.       |
| **Dropped Items** | Creates a distinct sound cue. | Drop items only when Cobb is far or distracted.          | Item swapping is more forgiving (v1.4), but sound still matters. |
| **Rocks**         | Can be thrown to create sound. | Use rocks as a distraction or to stun Cobb (v1.6).        | v1.6 made rock sounds louder, improving their utility.     |
| **Furnace/Breaker** | Interaction sounds are localized. | Engage with objectives only when the path is clear.      | No specific updates on sound, but interaction is inherent. |
| **Cobb's Movement** | Cobb's own sounds indicate proximity. | Listen carefully for Cobb's steps to gauge his location. | Cobb's pursuit speed adjusted in v1.6/v1.7.                 |

Rocks play a particularly interesting role when "Cobb Can Hear" is active. They can be thrown to create diversions, drawing Cobb away from your intended path. The v1.6 update made rock sounds louder, making them more effective for this purpose.

<Callout type="tip">
When "Cobb Can Hear" is active, rocks are your best friend. Use them to create distractions, lure Cobb away, or even stun him if he's in pursuit.
