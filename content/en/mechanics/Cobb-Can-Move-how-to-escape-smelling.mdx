<Section icon={Shield} title="Strategic Evasion Tactics for Smelling Cobb">
Escaping Cobb when his "Smell" ability is active requires a combination of careful movement, environmental awareness, and effective item usage. Unlike "See" or "Hear" rules, "Smell" bypasses line-of-sight and direct sound cues, making it a unique challenge.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Movement">
    When Cobb can smell, continuous movement is often safer than static hiding. Cobb will path towards your last known scented location, so staying still makes you an easier target. Keep moving, but do so methodically.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Map Layouts">
    Procedurally generated maps offer varied escape routes. Learn to recognize choke points, open areas, and loops. Use narrow corridors to your advantage, as Cobb might take longer to navigate them, giving you a chance to create distance.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Distract with Rocks">
    Rocks are a valuable tool. Throwing a rock can create a noise distraction, drawing Cobb away from your current position. This is particularly effective when you need to bypass a blocked path or create a momentary window for escape. V1.6 and v1.7 updates made rocks more effective, allowing direct hits to stun Cobb briefly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage Resources Efficiently">
    While not directly tied to smell, managing coal and batteries means you spend less time lingering in dangerous areas. Quick objective completion reduces exposure time to Cobb's active senses.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Adapt to Combined Rules">
    Sometimes, "Smell" might combine with other rules like "Reach" or "Duplicate." Be prepared to adjust your strategy. For instance, "Reach" means Cobb covers more ground per step, so maintaining greater distance is crucial.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Avoid panic-dropping items. While v1.4 made item swapping more forgiving, carelessly dropping items can alert Cobb to your presence or block your escape path, especially in tight situations.
</Callout>

</Section>
