<Section icon={Eye} title="Cobb's Sensory Abilities and Rule Breakdown">

The "mutations" in the game are officially referred to as rules. These rules dictate how Cobb interacts with the environment and the player. Starting from Level 2, the game guarantees at least one sensory rule, ensuring that players are immediately introduced to the core stealth mechanics.

| Mutation Name | Type | Effect on Cobb | Player Counter-Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cobb Can See** | Sensory | Detects player based on light level and distance | Stay in shadows; keep the furnace light low |
| **Cobb Can Hear** | Sensory | Attracted to running, dropping items, or rocks | Walk slowly; crouch-move; use rocks as distractions |
| **Cobb Can Smell** | Sensory | Periodically sniffs to find player's general area | Keep moving; do not stay in one corner for too long |
| **Reach** | Movement | Cobb takes larger, lunging steps during pursuit | Maintain extra distance; do not let him get close |
| **Duplicate** | Special | Creates a second Cobb that roams the map | Track both positions; avoid getting sandwiched |
| **Long Pursuit** | Behavior | Cobb gains speed the longer he chases you | Use rocks to break the chase; turn corners sharply |

<Callout type="warning" title="Smell Nerf in v1.7">
As of the v1.7 update in May 2026, the "Cobb can smell" mutation has been nerfed. Cobb now sniffs less frequently and is less persistent when he detects you at close range, giving you a better window to escape.
</Callout>

### Understanding Movement Mutations
Beyond senses, Cobb's physical behavior can mutate. The **Reach** rule is particularly dangerous because it changes the visual rhythm of his movement. While he moves slightly slower in terms of raw velocity, his "steps" are much larger, allowing him to close gaps unexpectedly. The **Duplicate** mutation was also improved in v1.6; duplicates now push each other instead of merging, making it easier to distinguish between multiple threats in tight hallways.

In Endless Mode, the "Starve" and "Freeze" rules become your biggest enemies alongside Cobb. These force you to stay mobile. You cannot simply hide in a corner; you must move to find carrots and coal. Use the "Seeded Randomness" mentioned by the developers to share interesting level layouts with the community or to practice specific challenging rule combinations.
