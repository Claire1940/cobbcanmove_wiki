<Section icon={Shield} title="Cobb's Sensory Abilities: See, Hear, Smell">
Cobb's primary threat comes from its sensory abilities, which are heavily influenced by the active rules. These rules dictate how Cobb detects the player, making careful movement and environmental awareness paramount. The game often guarantees a sensory rule by Level 2, ensuring players quickly learn to respect Cobb's detection methods.

### Cobb Can See
This rule makes Cobb detect the player based on their visibility and light level. Updates have actively balanced this ability, with v1.4 slightly nerfing its range and v1.5 adjusting it again. Players should treat "Cobb can see" as a major modifier to danger, requiring stealth and avoiding open areas.

### Cobb Can Smell
In v1.7, "Cobb can smell" received a nerf, resulting in less frequent sniffs and reduced persistence at close range. While less relentless than before, smell remains a critical sensory rule. Players should be mindful of their movements, as lingering in one spot can still attract Cobb.

### Cobb Can Hear
Although not explicitly detailed in recent patch notes for nerfs or buffs, "Cobb can hear" is a fundamental sensory rule. Loud movements or interactions can alert Cobb to the player's presence, making quiet navigation essential when this rule is active.

<Callout type="warning" title="Sensory Overload">
Ignoring sensory rules is a common beginner mistake. Each rule (See, Hear, Smell) demands a distinct approach to movement and interaction. Failing to adapt will lead to quick demise.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Cobb's Movement & Duplication Mechanics">
Beyond sensory abilities, Cobb's movement patterns and the dreaded duplication mechanic add layers of complexity to each run. Recent updates have refined these mechanics, influencing how players navigate the procedurally generated maps and engage with the monster.

### Pursuit Behavior
Cobb's pursuit speed has seen several adjustments. V1.6 made Cobb slightly faster during long pursuits, increasing pressure in extended chases. However, v1.7 slightly nerfed Cobb's acceleration during these long pursuits, making them a bit more manageable without removing the inherent danger. Random wandering speed in larger maps also increased in v1.6.

### Reach Rule
When the "Reach" rule is active, Cobb's movement changes significantly. V1.6 implemented a mechanic where Cobb takes larger steps but with slightly less overall speed. This alters the feel of Cobb's movement, requiring players to maintain greater spacing and anticipate larger lunges rather than just faster movement.

### Duplicate Cobbs
The duplication mechanic introduces multiple Cobbs to the map. V1.6 specifically addressed an issue where duplicate Cobbs could merge back into one, by making them push each other. This change ensures that duplicate Cobbs behave more distinctly and predictably, making multi-Cobb situations clearer and less confusing for the player. Duplicates can stack after Level 5 in Endless Mode.

<Callout type="success" title="Adapting to Cobb's Movements">
Mastering Cobb's movement patterns, especially under different rules, is a significant step towards consistent survival. Learn to recognize the visual and auditory cues for each ability.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Key Item Interactions & Environmental Mechanics">
Survival in Cobb Can Move isn't just about avoiding Cobb; it's also about effectively utilizing the environment and interactable items. Rocks, coal, carrots, and the crucial furnace and breaker mechanics all play a role in your survival loop. Recent updates have fine-tuned how these elements function, impacting player strategy.

### Rocks
Rocks are more than just environmental clutter. V1.6 made rocks capable of stunning Cobb for a short period on a direct hit and increased their sound clarity. V1.7 further solidified their utility by fixing instances where rocks failed to stun Cobb during pursuit. This makes rocks a reliable defensive tool in desperate situations.

### Coal and Furnaces
The core survival loop often involves collecting coal to power furnaces. V1.6 adjusted coal spawning, making it less likely to appear directly next to the furnace. This change increases the routing pressure and forces players to move further, adding to the challenge. The Blast Furnace in the ending also received a larger hitbox in v1.6 for easier interaction.

### Breakers and Batteries
The breaker is a visible map object players need to recognize. V1.3 updated its sprite to make it more noticeable. Batteries are essential for progression, specifically tied to the ending sequence. V1.4 changed the final task text after placing four batteries, and the overall final task balance was tweaked in v1.4 and v1.5.2.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Mastering Survival: Advanced Tips & Strategies">
To truly master Cobb Can Move, players must integrate their understanding of Cobb's mechanics with strategic gameplay. This involves not only reacting to immediate threats but also planning for future challenges and leveraging game features like death tips and level select.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Rule Awareness">
    Before making any significant move, always confirm the active rule. This dictates Cobb's primary detection method and should heavily influence your route planning and item usage. A "Cobb can see" rule demands darkness and cover, while "Cobb can smell" requires rapid movement through areas.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Strategic Rock Usage">
    Rocks are your most reliable counter-play tool. Use them not just as a last resort, but proactively to create diversions or stun Cobb for crucial seconds. Practice direct hits to maximize their stun potential, especially during pursuits where Cobb's speed can be overwhelming.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Efficient Resource Management">
    Coal is your lifeline. Plan routes that efficiently gather coal while minimizing exposure to Cobb. Remember that coal spawns are now further from furnaces, so factor in extra travel time. Carrots respawn, providing a sustainable food source if you can safely reach them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn from Every Death">
    The v1.4 update introduced death tips. These are invaluable learning tools. Instead of immediately restarting, take a moment to read the tip. It often highlights a specific mechanic or mistake relevant to your last run, helping you refine your strategy.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Utilize Level Select for Practice">
    After completing the story mode, the v1.5 update unlocks a level select menu. Use this to practice difficult levels or specific rule combinations. This focused practice can significantly improve your ability to handle various Cobb mechanics without the pressure of a full run.
  </Step>
</Steps>
