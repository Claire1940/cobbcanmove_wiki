<Section icon={Shield} title="Cobb Can Move jumpscare: Mechanics of Terror">

Surviving the **Cobb Can Move jumpscare** requires a deep understanding of the game's rule-shifting engine. Developed by abho and available on the [official itch.io page](https://abho.itch.io/cobb-can-move), this retro pixel horror game places you in dark, procedurally generated levels where the monster's capabilities shift dynamically. The jumpscare itself is triggered when the crawling red entity, Cobb, corners and grabs your character. 

In earlier versions of the game, a notable bug sometimes allowed players to survive a physical grab without triggering the death sequence. However, updates in 2026 resolved this issue. If Cobb makes physical contact with your hitbox, the screen locks, a high-contrast pixel animation plays, and you are sent back to the main menu. 

| Sensation Rule | Jumpscare Trigger Cause | Prevention Method |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Cobb can see** | Holding active light source in Cobb's line of sight | Douse light or hide behind solid wall chunks |
| **Cobb can hear** | Running, throwing rocks, or dropping items nearby | Move slowly (walk) and time item drops |
| **Cobb can smell** | Idle player standing still for too long | Relocate constantly; smell nerfs in v1.7 reduce tracking persistence |
| **Reach** | Close-quarters navigation near Cobb | Maintain a minimum spacing of 3+ tiles; watch out for large steps |

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Death Mechanic Update">
Since the v1.4 patch, the game features dynamic death tips. When you fail a level, the game displays a contextual hint that explains which active rule you violated, helping you adjust your strategy for the next attempt.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Map} title="Dynamic Rules: Sensory Behaviors & Cobb Senses">

The primary hook of this pixel horror experience is that you cannot rely on a single, static survival strategy. Every level selects random rules that dictate how Cobb detects you. If you treat a level where "Cobb can see" the same as one where "Cobb can smell," you will trigger the **Cobb Can Move jumpscare** almost immediately.

Understanding how these senses operate in the procedurally generated environments is key to navigating the dark hallways safely.

  <Card title="Visual Tracking">

    - Turning on your flashlight within his line of sight instantly alerts him.

  </Card>
  <Card title="Acoustic Tracking">
    - **Cobb can hear** rule penalizes loud movements.

    - Sneaking and careful resource management are mandatory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Olfactory Tracking">
    - **Cobb can smell** rule makes hiding in one spot impossible.
    - Cobb slowly tracks your historical path, sniffing you out over time.

  </Card>

During long chases, Cobb gets progressively faster. The v1.6 update implemented a pursuit acceleration mechanic, though it was slightly dialed back in the v1.7 patch to keep chases fair. Additionally, when the "Reach" rule is active, Cobb changes his movement animation to take larger steps. This reduces his base speed but allows him to close short gaps instantly, catching unprepared players by surprise.

<Callout type="tip" title="Guaranteed Level 2 Rule">
The v1.4 update guarantees that Level 2 will always feature a sensory rule (either hear, smell, or see). Use this level to learn the baseline behavior of Cobb's tracking systems before additional mechanics load in later stages.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Items Guide: Furnace, Coal, and Defensive Utility">

To keep Cobb at bay and prevent the darkness from crushing you, you must interact with various items scattered across the procedural layout. The core gameplay loop involves feeding the central furnace with coal, maintaining the breaker boxes, and collecting defensive rocks.

| Item Name | Role & Function | Update Note (2026) | Tactical Takeaway |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Coal** | Central survival fuel for the furnace. | v1.6 prevents spawns directly beside the furnace. | Expect to travel further into dark corridors to locate fuel. |
| **Rocks** | Throwables used for distraction and self-defense. | v1.6 added short stun; v1.7 fixed pursuit stun bug. | Throw directly at Cobb's face during chases to buy escape time. |
| **Carrots** | Stamina-restoring food items. | v1.3 enabled carrot respawns when consumed. | Re-visit harvested carrot patches if your stamina bar runs low. |
| **Batteries** | Progression items required to unlock final stages. | v1.4 altered final task text after placing 4 units. | Prioritize battery collection early before Cobb's speed scales. |
| **Breaker** | Map objective to restore lighting. | v1.3 updated the sprite asset to make it easier to see. | Always map out the breaker location in case the fuse blows. |

Managing your inventory in tight corridors can be stressful, especially when Cobb is closing in. Fortunately, quality-of-life updates have streamlined item handling.

<Callout type="success" title="Inventory Handling Buff">
Beginning in v1.4, you can swap items even if the drop spot is highlighted in red over a wall. This prevents inventory lockups when backed into tight corners.
</Callout>

Survival in Endless Mode relies entirely on real-time adaptability and threat prioritization.

In Endless Mode, duplicate Cobbs can spawn to double the threat. In older versions, these duplicates could merge back into a single sprite, causing visual bugs and sudden jumpscares. The v1.6 update resolved this by forcing duplicate Cobbs to physically push each other away, keeping their hitboxes separate and clear.

<Callout type="warning" title="Endless Mode Starting Level">
