<Section icon={Target} title="Cobb's Abilities: Theoretical Framework and Practical Impact">

Cobb's terrifying effectiveness stems from its varied and adaptable abilities. Understanding the theoretical underpinnings of each ability—and how they interact with the game's changing rules—is crucial for survival. These abilities are not static; recent updates have consistently tweaked their balance and behavior.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sensory Abilities">
    - **Cobb Can See**: Detects players based on light level and direct line of sight. v1.4 and v1.5 adjusted its range, making it less oppressive but still dangerous.
    - **Cobb Can Hear**: Reacts to player-generated sounds. Requires careful, quiet movement.
    - **Cobb Can Smell**: Pursues players based on scent trails. v1.7 nerfed its persistence and frequency, but it remains a potent tracking tool.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Physical & Replication Abilities">
    - **Cobb Can Move**: Basic movement, but pursuit speed can accelerate during long chases (v1.6, slightly nerfed in v1.7).
    - **Cobb Can Reach**: Increases Cobb's attack range, requiring greater spacing. v1.6 changed this to larger steps at reduced speed.
    - **Cobb Can Duplicate**: Creates copies, especially after Level 5. v1.6 fixed duplicates merging by making them push each other, ensuring distinct threats.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

The interaction between these abilities and the random rule selection forms the core of Cobb Can Move's emergent gameplay. For instance, a "Cobb Can Smell" rule combined with "Long Pursuit Speed" creates a significantly different challenge than "Cobb Can See" in a dark environment.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Tactics: Adapting to Procedural Generation and Dynamic Rules">

Effective survival in Cobb Can Move isn't about memorization; it's about dynamic adaptation. The game's procedural generation ensures unique map layouts, while the rule-changing system demands flexible strategies. Here's a breakdown of tactics to navigate this ever-shifting horror.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Rule Assessment">
    Upon entering a new level, immediately identify the active rule. This dictates your primary threat and necessary adjustments to movement and resource use. For example, a "Cobb Can Hear" rule demands quiet movement, while "Cobb Can See" necessitates staying out of sightlines.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Master Resource Management">
    **Coal** is central to objectives. **Rocks** can distract or stun Cobb (v1.6, v1.7 fixes). **Carrots** provide sustenance and now respawn (v1.3). Efficiently gather and deploy these items based on the current situation and Cobb's active abilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Environmental Elements">
    The map features **furnaces** for coal delivery, **breakers** to interact with (v1.3 made them more noticeable), and various obstacles. Learn how to use these elements to your advantage, creating barriers or escape routes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Learn from Death Tips">
    v1.4 introduced death tips, providing valuable feedback on what went wrong. Analyze these tips to refine your approach for subsequent runs, turning failure into a learning opportunity.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Adapt to Cobb's Pursuit">
    Cobb's pursuit behavior varies. Long pursuits can make Cobb faster (v1.6), though v1.7 slightly nerfed acceleration. Understand when to break line of sight, use distractions, or commit to a sprint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Adaptive Play">
The most successful players are those who can quickly assess the current rules and adapt their movement, resource use, and interaction with the environment.
</Callout>
