<Section icon={Map} title="The Origin of Cobb and the Shifting Dungeon Lore">

The **Cobb Can Move lore** is defined by a singular, terrifying concept: the environment is alive and constantly rewriting its own rules. Set in a dark, pixelated abyss, players find themselves trapped in a procedural dungeon where the laws of physics and monster behavior are never static. Unlike traditional horror games where a monster follows a fixed path, Cobb is an adaptive predator. This lore is deeply rooted in the "Major Jam 7" winning concept, where the "limitation" of the game became its greatest narrative strength.

In this world, the player is not just a victim but a participant in a cycle of heat and cold. The dungeon demands coal to keep the furnace burning, suggesting a world that has long since frozen over or descended into a sunless void. The presence of rocks, carrots, and industrial machinery like breakers and batteries hints at a previous civilization that attempted to contain or coexist with the entity known as Cobb.

<Section icon={Target} title="Cobb's Sensory Abilities and Behavioral Lore">

Cobb is not just a monster; he is a manifestation of the dungeon's rules. His physical form—a small red creature with disproportionately long arms—allows him to reach across gaps and grab players from a distance. The **Cobb Can Move lore** explains that his power fluctuates based on the active level rule. When a sensory rule is active, Cobb's connection to the player's world strengthens, allowing him to detect sounds, light, or even scent.

<Callout type="tip" title="Survival Strategy">
Always check the rule text at the top of the screen. Level 2 is guaranteed to introduce a sensory rule, which is the dungeon's way of testing your ability to hide your presence from Cobb.

The lore also mentions "Duplicates." After Level 5, Cobb can split into multiple entities. This isn't just a gameplay mechanic; it represents the dungeon's attempt to overwhelm the player as they get closer to the exit. According to official devlogs from abho, these duplicates now push each other instead of merging, making the threat feel more like a pack of hunters.

If the furnace goes out, the "Freeze" rule eventually takes hold, representing the absolute zero temperature of the abyss. The coal you collect is the lifeblood of this industrial tomb.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Story Mode Progression and the Final Task">

The story mode of Cobb Can Move follows a progression of increasing complexity. As you move from Level 1 to the finale, the dungeon throws more rules at you simultaneously. The lore culminates in the "Final Task," a sequence that requires the player to find and place four batteries to power the blast furnace and escape.

The ending of the game is bittersweet. While you may escape the immediate dungeon, the developer abho includes a message promoting their next project, *VOIDFACE*, hinting that the horrors of the pixelated abyss may be part of a much larger universe of survival horror.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Endless Mode and the Future of the Abyss">

For those who survive the story, Endless Mode offers a deeper look into the **Cobb Can Move lore**. Here, the dungeon has no end, and the rules continue to stack until survival becomes impossible. This mode represents the true nature of the abyss—a place of infinite, shifting torment.

In Endless Mode, the "Seed" system allows players to share specific dungeon layouts. This community aspect has led to the discovery of "cursed" seeds where Cobb's duplicate rules trigger early, creating a chaotic environment that challenges even the most veteran players. The 2026 v1.7 update specifically targeted this mode, nerfing Cobb's acceleration during long pursuits to make high-level play more about skill and less about inevitable death.
