<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Essentials: Beginner Guide">

Surviving your first few runs in the **Cobb Can Move short game** requires more than just fast reflexes. You must become a master of the environment. Every level starts with a text prompt that dictates Cobb's current ability. Ignoring this text is the most common cause of death for new players.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Active Rule">
    Before moving, read the text at the top of the screen. If it says "Cobb can see," stay out of his direct line of sight. If it says "Cobb can smell," keep moving to avoid a scent trail.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Resource Prioritization">
    Your primary goal is to keep the furnace burning. Locate coal chunks and bring them to the central furnace. In v1.6, coal spawning was adjusted to be further from the furnace, increasing the danger of long treks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Breaker and Visibility">
    If the lights flicker or go out, find the Breaker. The sprite was updated in v1.3 to be more visible. Keeping the lights on prevents the "Freeze" rule from draining your health.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Defensive Rock Tactics">
    Always carry a rock. Since the v1.6 and v1.7 updates, a direct hit with a rock will stun Cobb. This is your only hard reset during a pursuit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Death is a Teacher">
The v1.4 update added **Death Tips**. If you die, don't skip the screen immediately. The game analyzes how you died and provides a hint specific to the rule that caught you.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Story Mode Progression and Ending Walkthrough">

The story mode of the **Cobb Can Move short game** consists of seven levels leading to a climactic finale. Each level introduces more complex rule combinations, culminating in a resource-heavy final task.

**Level-by-Level Breakdown:**
- **Level 1-2**: Introduction to basic movement. Level 2 is guaranteed to feature a sensory rule (See, Hear, or Smell) to teach detection mechanics.
- **Level 3-6**: Introduction of "Duplicate" and "Reach" rules. Maps become larger and more procedural.
- **Level 7**: The "Gauntlet." v1.5 added extra rocks to this level to compensate for Cobb's increased aggression.
- **The Ending**: Requires placing four batteries and interacting with the **Blast Furnace**. The v1.6 update enlarged the furnace hitbox, making it much easier to click under pressure.

<Callout type="warning" title="Ending Difficulty">
Cobb's speed during the final battery task was slightly buffed in v1.5.2. Do not attempt to place the final battery if Cobb is within three tiles of you.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Endless Mode and Replay Strategy">

Once you have mastered the story, the **Cobb Can Move short game** offers an Endless Mode that tests your adaptability to its limit. This mode starts at Level 3, skipping the introductory phases and throwing you straight into the deep end of procedural generation.

**Endless Mode Features:**
- **Seeded Randomness**: Every run uses a unique seed, ensuring that item placements and map chunks are never identical.
- **Scaling Pressure**: As levels progress, Cobb gains speed faster, and the "Starve" and "Freeze" timers become more aggressive.
- **Duplicate Stacking**: Unlike story mode, Endless can feature more than two Cobbs simultaneously in later stages.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Replay Tip">
Use the **Level Select** menu (unlocked after your first clear) to practice Level 7 specifically. It is the best way to learn how to dodge Cobb while carrying heavy batteries.
</Callout>
