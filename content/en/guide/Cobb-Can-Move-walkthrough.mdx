<Section icon={Shield} title="Understanding Changing Rules and Cobb Abilities">

The core mechanic of the game is that the rules change every level. You cannot rely on a single strategy because what worked in Level 1 might get you killed in Level 2. The game notifies you of the active rule at the start of the level. Ignoring this text is the most common reason for player failure in any Cobb Can Move walkthrough.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Sensory Rules">
    - **Cobb Can See**: Sight is based on your light level. Stay in shadows to remain hidden.
    - **Cobb Can Hear**: Noise from running or throwing rocks alerts him to your exact location.
    - **Cobb Can Smell**: A persistent tracking rule that was nerfed in v1.7 to be less relentless at close range.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Movement & Behavior">
    - **Reach Rule**: Cobb takes larger steps but moves with less overall speed, requiring more spacing.
    - **Duplicate Rule**: Multiple Cobbs appear. They push each other rather than merging, increasing the danger area.
    - **Long Pursuit**: Cobb gains acceleration during extended chases, though this was slightly reduced in recent patches.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Survival depends on your ability to adapt. If "Cobb Can Hear" is active, you must walk instead of run. If "Cobb Can See" is active, you must manage the breaker and furnace light carefully to avoid being spotted from across the room.

<Callout type="warning" title="Rule Priority">
Level 2 is guaranteed to introduce a sensory rule. Treat this as your training ground for learning how to break line-of-sight and manage noise levels before the map complexity increases in later stages.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Story Mode Progression and Level 7 Strategy">

The journey through story mode consists of seven levels of increasing difficulty. Early levels focus on single rules, while later levels combine sensory detection with environmental hazards like freezing or starving. Level 7 is the ultimate test of your skills, requiring you to locate four batteries while managing a highly aggressive Cobb.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Scouting">
    Locate the furnace and the nearest coal spawns. Do not pick up batteries yet; focus on establishing a safe zone with enough light to see incoming threats.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Battery Collection">
    Search the procedural chunks for four batteries. Carry them one by one back to the central objective area. Use rocks to distract Cobb if he camps near a battery spawn.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Final Task Activation">
    Once all four batteries are placed, the final task begins. The text will change, and Cobb will receive a speed buff. This is the most dangerous part of the game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Escape">
    Navigate to the blast furnace. The hitbox was enlarged in v1.6 to make it easier to interact with under pressure. Complete the interaction to trigger the ending sequence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

After beating the game once, you unlock the Level Select menu. This is useful for practicing specific rule combinations or revisiting Level 7 to refine your speedrun tactics. If you die, pay close attention to the death tips added in v1.4, as they often reveal exactly which rule you violated.

<Callout type="info" title="Level 7 Balance">
The final task was rebalanced in v1.5.2 to increase Cobb's speed slightly. You must be efficient with your movement; any wasted time during the battery phase will make the final escape significantly harder.
</Callout>

</Section>
